# Suggested Reviewers — TNNLS Submission

**Title:** Critical Bandwidth Modality Testing for Estimating the Number of Clusters

---

## Primary Area: Cluster Validation and CVIs

1. **Prof. Olatz Arbelaitz**
   - Department of Computer Science, University of the Basque Country
   - Email: o.arbelaitz@ehu.eus
   - Expertise: Extensive comparative studies of cluster validity indices (Arbelaitz et al., Pattern Recognition 2013)
   - Rationale: Leading authority on CVI benchmarking methodology

2. **Prof. Christian Hennig**
   - Department of Statistical Sciences, University of Bologna
   - Email: christian.hennig@unibo.it
   - Expertise: Foundations of clustering, true clusters concept, feature rescaling
   - Rationale: Deep expertise in what constitutes a "true cluster" and CVI limitations

## Secondary Area: Modality Testing and Kernel Density Estimation

3. **Prof. Jose E. Chacón**
   - Department of Mathematics, Universidad de Extremadura
   - Email: jechacon@unex.es
   - Expertise: Mode clustering theory, density-based clustering foundations
   - Rationale: Leading authority on the theoretical connection between density modes and clusters

4. **Prof. Bernhard W. Silverman**
   - (Retired, formerly Princeton / Oxford)
   - Email: silverman@stat.oxford.ac.uk
   - Expertise: Critical bandwidth testing, kernel density estimation
   - Rationale: Originator of critical bandwidth modality testing framework

## Tertiary Area: Benchmarking and Evaluation Methodology

5. **Prof. Janez Demšar**
   - Faculty of Computer and Information Science, University of Ljubljana
   - Email: janez.demsar@fri.uni-lj.si
   - Expertise: Statistical comparison of classifiers/indices across multiple datasets
   - Rationale: Authority on appropriate statistical testing methodology (Friedman, Nemenyi, Wilcoxon-Holm)

6. **Prof. Ulrike von Luxburg**
   - Department of Computer Science, University of Tübingen
   - Email: ulrike.luxburg@uni-tuebingen.de

   - Rationale: Deep perspective on cluster validation as a field
